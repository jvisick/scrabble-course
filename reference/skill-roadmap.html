The 5 Pillars of Competitive Scrabble

What separates a casual player from a strong one — and the order we'll build it.

Casual Scrabble is a vocabulary game: play the best word you can see. Competitive Scrabble is a tile-management and board-geometry game that happens to use words. These five pillars are the whole of it. We build them in order, but interleave — a little of pillar 2 starts before pillar 1 is finished, because mixing skills is what makes them durable. (Chang, CMU 98-252 Wordplay.)

1 Words Started

The raw material. Not "big vocabulary" — a specific, ranked set of legal plays.

Path: 2-letter words (107) → 3-letter words → the JQXZ words → 4-letter words → bingo stems. Learn meanings, not just letters — meaning is the memory hook.

Payoff: play into tight boards, unlock parallel plays and hooks, never miss a legal move.

2 The Leave Up next

What you keep matters as much as what you play. After every move you leave tiles on your rack — a balanced leave (≈3 vowels / 4 consonants, keeping S and blanks) sets up your next turn. (Rack management.)

Key ideas: vowel-consonant balance · tile turnover (play more tiles to draw the good ones) · never break up a near-bingo for a few points · equity (a play's true value = score + how good it leaves you).

Payoff: stop making the "high-scoring" play that wrecks your next three turns.

3 Board Vision Later

Reading the board as geometry: where the points and the dangers are.

Key ideas: hooks · parallel plays · using premium squares (put a high tile on a DL/TL) · blocking the board when you're ahead · not opening a TW lane for your opponent.

Payoff: score more with the same tiles, and deny your opponent theirs.

4 Bingos Later

Playing all 7 tiles for a +50 bonus — the biggest single swing in the game. Strong players average 1.5–2+ per game.

Key ideas: the "clean" 6-tile stems (SATIRE / RETINA, TISANE, SANE + 3rd letter…) that combine with one more tile into many 7s · anagramming under a clock · recognizing bingo-prone racks and holding for them.

Payoff: the 50-point bonus that decides most games between good players.

5 Game Management Later

Running the whole game, not just the current move.

Key ideas: when to exchange instead of playing · when to sacrifice score for a better leave · fishing for a bingo · basic endgame (counting the unseen tiles, playing out for the win).

Payoff: convert small edges into wins; stop losing games you were winning.
