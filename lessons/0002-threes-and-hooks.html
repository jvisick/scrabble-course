Grow the 2s: Your First Hooks

You spent Lesson 01 learning 107 small words. Today you learn that most of the 3s are hiding inside them.

Today's win: learn to hook — grow a word already on the board by one letter — and walk away knowing the answer to the single most game-useful question about the 2s: does it take an S?

One letter, whole new word

A hook is a single letter added to the front or back of a word on the board to make a new word. Add to the front (H + AT → HAT): a front hook. Add to the end (AT + E → ATE): a back hook.

Here's why this is the natural next step after the 2s: of the 1,085 three-letter words in NWL2023, 850 — nearly 8 in 10 — are a two-letter word plus one hook. You are not starting a new list today. You're extending one you already own. And the short words are the game: about 75% of all words played come from the 2s, 3s, and 4s (Baron, Scrabble Tips).

The payoff isn't the three points. A hook is a door: it lets you attach a whole new word of your own to a board that looked closed. The classic move — hook an S onto something, and play your own word through that S:

The S-hook play The board has QI on it. You hold an S, a U, and an N. Drop the S after QI and run SUN downward from it:
QIS U N
One S, two scores: QIS (12) and SUN — in a spot where nothing else fit. This works with any back hook: the hooked letter becomes the first (or last) letter of your own perpendicular word. But it only works if QIS is actually a word. Which brings us to…

The S-question

S is the best hooking letter in the game — so good that Baron lists the four S's with the blanks, J, Q, X and Z as the ten power tiles (Scrabble Tips, §2d). But it doesn't work everywhere, and guessing wrong costs you a turn:

  • 75 of the 107 two-letter words take a final S: QI→QIS, ZA→ZAS, KA→KAS, even AA→AAS.
  • 32 refuse it. These are the traps — they feel pluralizable:
The 32 that refuse an S — NWL2023

AE AM AN AW AX DE EH ET EW EX HM IS JO LO MM MY NA NE OF OI OK OS OW OX OY SH TO UH US WE XU YO

The famous ones: JO pluralizes as JOES, not JOS. AX and OX need four letters (AXES, OXEN). And XU is the loneliest word in the list — the only 2 with no hooks at all, front or back.

Don't memorize all 32 tonight. Today's drill plants the high-traffic ones; the hook chart is your daily sheet for the rest.

Front hooks: the ones hiding in plain sight

Back hooks get the glory because of S, but front hooks are the sneaky ones — everyday words you never noticed were a 2 wearing a hat. Tap each to check the breakdown:

SHE
WHO
TWO
GNU
CHI
BAA
UDO
SPA

This is the skill to carry into your games this week: every time you see a two-letter word on the board, ask "what letters grow this?" — front and back.

Your daily habit this week (5 min) Alternate days: the Lesson 01 drill one day, the 2s-to-make-3s hook chart the next. On chart days, cover the right column with your hand and quiz yourself: "AG — does it take an S? What else?" Mixing the two lists (interleaving) is deliberately harder than drilling one — and that difficulty is what makes both stick.
